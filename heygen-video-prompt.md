## Full brief (for script/narrative)

**Video goal:** A 2–4 minute explainer for freight brokers, carriers, and shippers that shows what IronFreight is, why it exists, and how it works from first load to verified delivery.

**Tone:** Professional, confident, and clear. You’re the trusted partner, not the salesperson.

**Script / narrative outline:**

1. **The problem (0:00–0:30)**  
   “In freight, double brokering, load theft, and identity fraud cost the industry billions. Shippers and brokers need to know exactly who picked up and delivered their freight, and that the driver at the dock is the same carrier they contracted—not a double broker or a thief. Today we’ll show how IronFreight solves that from start to finish.”

2. **What IronFreight is (0:30–0:50)**  
   “IronFreight is a verification and chain-of-custody platform. We don’t move freight—we verify who moved it, where, and when. We give shippers, brokers, and carriers one source of truth: the right driver, at the right place, at the right time, with proof.”

3. **Who’s in the system (0:50–1:15)**  
   “Four parties use IronFreight: the **shipper** (who has the freight), the **broker** (who books the load), the **carrier** (who assigns a driver), and the **driver** (who runs the load). Everyone gets a role-based view. Brokers and carriers manage loads and see the full chain of custody. Drivers get a simple app: their assigned load, pickup and delivery addresses, and a secure QR code they show at the dock.”

4. **Flow from start to finish (1:15–2:45)**  
   - “A broker or carrier creates the load in IronFreight and assigns a driver.  
   - The driver logs into the IronFreight Driver app—on their phone or as an installed app—and sees their one assigned load: pickup and delivery addresses, appointment windows, and a link to open directions in maps.  
   - At pickup, the driver opens the app and shows the **IronGate QR code**. The shipper or dock scans it. That scan is **geofenced** and **timestamped**—so we know the driver was at the right location at the right time. That’s pickup verified.  
   - The driver then runs the load. At delivery, they show the same kind of QR again. The receiving party scans it. Delivery verified.  
   - From the broker or carrier dashboard, you see the full chain: who had the load, when they were at origin and destination, and that verification came from a scan at the facility—not just a checkbox. No more ‘trust me’—you get proof.”

5. **Why it matters (2:45–3:15)**  
   “Drivers only see what they need: addresses and appointments. We hide pay, broker name, and shipper contact so the platform protects margins and prevents back-solicitation. Shippers and brokers get a clear, auditable chain of custody and protection against double brokering and load theft. Carriers get a professional verification story to win and keep freight.”

6. **Close (3:15–3:30)**  
   “That’s IronFreight: one system, one chain of custody, from load assignment to verified pickup and delivery. If you’re ready to add an ironclad layer to your freight operations, we’re here to help.”

**Visual / scene suggestions:**  
- Problem: quick cuts of “who really has my load?” / paperwork / fraud headlines.  
- Product: show the broker/carrier dashboard (loads, assignments), then the driver app (single load, addresses, “Show IronGate QR”), then a shipper scanning the QR.  
- End: logo, tagline (“The Ironclad Shield Against Double Brokering”), and a clear CTA (e.g. “Get a quote at getironfreight.com” or “Contact us”).

## Short one-prompt version (paste into HeyGen as a single prompt)

Create a 3-minute professional explainer video for IronFreight, a freight verification and chain-of-custody platform that prevents double brokering and load theft. Script outline: (1) Open with the problem—double brokering, fraud, and lack of proof of who picked up and delivered freight. (2) Introduce IronFreight as the verification layer that gives shippers, brokers, carriers, and drivers one source of truth. (3) Explain the four roles—shipper, broker, carrier, driver—and that drivers get a simple app with one assigned load and a secure QR code. (4) Walk through the full flow: broker/carrier creates and assigns the load; driver uses the IronFreight Driver app to see pickup/delivery and show the IronGate QR at origin and destination; shipper/receiver scan the QR for geofenced, timestamped verification; broker/carrier sees the full chain of custody. (5) Emphasize that driver view is restricted to protect margins and prevent back-solicitation, while shippers and brokers get auditable proof. (6) Close with the tagline “The Ironclad Shield Against Double Brokering” and a CTA to get a quote at getironfreight.com. Tone: confident, clear, and professional. Suggest visuals: dashboard, driver app, QR scan at dock, chain-of-custody view.
